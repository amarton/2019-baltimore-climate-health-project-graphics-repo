As the temperatures increases, EMS calls for many heat-related illnesses rise

Hot temperatures are grouped into four categories: "not safe" for temps above 80 degrees, "cation" for temps ranging from 80 - 89 degrees, "extreme caution" for temps from 90 - 102 degrees and "danger" for temps over 103 degrees. This chart shows the rise and fall of emergency medical calls for certain health conditions in Baltimore in 2018 in each of these four categories. Each bar represents the number of hours between calls for a particular health condition in each category. For example, calls for hyperthermia, dehydration and asthma all increase dramatically when temps raise from "not safe" to "danger." Select the button for each category to explore how hours between EMS calls changed at different temperatures.

By Amina Lampkin and Sean Mussenden

Source: Baltimore EMS calls 2014-2018, data obtained from Baltimore City Fire Department
